# United Developers — Roofing Sales Rep Playbook & Ramp Academy
### From hire to first signed contract in 2–4 weeks

This is the field manual every new rep gets on day one. Pair it with **Agent 3 (AI Sales Training Simulator)** for daily role-play and call review. The goal: a coachable rep producing signed, collected deals fast.

---

## 1. The UD Offer in one breath
"We're United Developers — GAF-certified, A+ BBB, 131+ five-star reviews. We do a **free roof inspection**, document storm damage properly, **handle your insurance claim with you**, and most homeowners end up paying **only their deductible**. Most roofs go on in **one day**, with a **lifetime material warranty**."

Memorize that. Everything else is detail.

---

## 2. The Sales Process (7 stages)
1. **Knock / contact** → get permission to inspect.
2. **Inspect** → climb, document hail/wind damage with photos (don't sell yet — gather).
3. **Present findings** → show the homeowner exactly what you found.
4. **Position insurance** → explain the claim path and "deductible only."
5. **Book the adjuster / file** → set the next concrete step with a date.
6. **Advocate at adjuster meeting** → make sure legitimate scope is captured.
7. **Sign & schedule install** → contract, then production takes over.

**Golden rule:** every interaction ends with a *scheduled* next step. No "I'll think about it" without a date.

---

## 3. Door-Knock Scripts

**Opener (storm area):**
> "Hey, I'm [Name] with United Developers — we're the roofing company working here in [neighborhood] after the [hail/wind] that came through. We're doing free roof inspections for your neighbors this week. I'm not here to sell you anything today — I just want to get up there, take some photos, and let you know if you've got damage worth a claim. Takes about 20 minutes. Mind if I take a quick look?"

**If "I'm busy":**
> "Totally fair — I won't take your time. The inspection doesn't even need you there. Can I grab your number and book a 20-minute slot when it's convenient? If there's no damage, I'll tell you straight."

**If "My roof is fine":**
> "Hope so — and a lot look fine from the ground. Hail damage usually hides up top. The inspection's free and you'll have photos either way for your records. Worst case, you get peace of mind. Want me to take a look while I'm here?"

**If "Are you trying to get me to file a claim?":**
> "Only if you actually have legitimate damage — I'll never push a claim that isn't real. If you do, I'll document it properly and walk you through the whole thing. If you don't, I'll tell you and you owe me nothing."

## 4. Insurance Conversation (the part that closes)

Plain-English explanations every rep must nail:

- **Deductible:** "It's the part you pay. On an approved storm claim, that's usually all you pay — insurance covers the rest of a new roof."
- **ACV vs RCV:** "ACV is the depreciated value they pay first. RCV is the full replacement cost — you get the rest once the work is done. We help you collect both checks."
- **"Will my premium go up?":** "Storm/weather claims are 'acts of God' — they're treated differently from at-fault claims. Many carriers don't surcharge for them, but check your policy. Either way, an unrepaired roof costs more long-term."
- **"What if they deny it?":** "Then you've lost nothing — the inspection was free. But we document it properly the first time, which is exactly why claims get approved."

**Never:** promise approval, invent damage, or quote a payout. Legitimate claims only — it protects the homeowner, your commission, and the company license.

## 5. Objection Handling Cheat Sheet

| Objection | Response framework |
|---|---|
| "I need to talk to my spouse." | "Smart — big decision. Let's get you both on a 10-min call tonight so you're deciding together with all the info. 6 or 7pm?" |
| "I want 3 more quotes." | "Good idea to compare. Just compare apples to apples — GAF certification, warranty, and who handles your claim. Most reps just sell you a roof; we manage the whole insurance side. Can I leave you a one-pager to compare against?" |
| "Your competitor is cheaper." | "On a claim, the price is mostly set by your insurance scope, not us — so 'cheaper' often means they're cutting the scope or the materials. We make sure the full legitimate scope is captured, which protects you." |
| "I don't have time for a claim." | "That's exactly what we handle — we meet the adjuster, do the paperwork, and keep it moving. Your time is one signature and one inspection." |
| "Let me think about it." | "Of course. What's the one thing you're unsure about — the damage, the process, or the company? Let's solve that now so 'thinking about it' is actually a yes or no." |

Drill each of these with **Agent 3** until they're automatic.

## 6. Activity Math (what produces $250K+)
- Talk to enough doors → inspections → claims filed → approvals → installs.
- Track daily: doors knocked, inspections set, inspections done, claims filed, contracts signed.
- A producing rep controls **activity**, not outcomes. Outcomes follow activity.
- Commission ladder: **10%** → **12%** after $250K collected → **15%** after $500K collected (paid 50% ACV / 50% RCV).

## 7. Daily Rhythm of a Top Rep
- **Morning:** review pipeline + today's booked inspections; 15-min AI role-play drill on your weakest area.
- **Midday:** inspections, adjuster meetings, supplements.
- **Late afternoon / evening:** door-knock active ZIPs while homeowners are home.
- **End of day:** log everything in CRM; submit one real call to Agent 3 for review.

## 8. The 2–4 Week Ramp (graduation gates)
- **Week 1 — Foundations.** Product, damage ID, openers, booking. *Gate:* score 8/10 on door-knock role-play ×3.
- **Week 2 — Inspection + Insurance.** Present findings, explain ACV/RCV/deductible. *Gate:* clean insurance explanation + 1 booked adjuster meeting.
- **Week 3 — Objections + Close.** *Gate:* 7/10 on the close role-play ×3 and pass the adjuster simulation.
- **Week 4 — Live production.** Ride-alongs → self-gen deals → daily call review. *Gate:* **first signed contract.**

## 9. Manager's Weekly Coaching Loop
1. Pull each rep's Agent 3 scorecards + real activity numbers.
2. Identify the single biggest bottleneck per rep (discovery? close? insurance?).
3. Assign one targeted drill for the week.
4. Celebrate wins publicly; coach gaps privately.
5. Review the leaderboard: doors → inspections → signed → collected.
