Objection handlers

"My roof's fine."
"From the driveway it always looks fine — the crease is up at the seal line, you can't see it from down here. That's exactly why the look is free. Twenty minutes and you'll know for sure."
"My rates will go up."
"Storm claims are Act-of-God — carriers rate the whole region, not you personally. The storm already happened; your rate moves either way. Not filing just leaves money on the table."
"I need to talk to my spouse."
"Totally — what would they want to know? Let's get the facts photographed now so you're showing them something real, and I'll set a time you're both home."
"Getting other quotes."
"Smart. One thing most folks don't know: on a claim the insurance scope sets the price — a cheaper bid usually means they cut scope or skip code items. Compare who fights for the full scope, not who's cheapest."
"What's it cost?"
"If it's storm damage, your number is your deductible — usually a grand or two, insurance covers the rest. If it's not, you get a real written quote instead of a guess. Free either way. And we've got $0-down financing if you want it."
"Not interested."
"Fair enough — I'll leave you my card. One thing: claims have about a one-year window from the storm. If you wait and it starts leaking this winter, that window may be closed. The free look protects you either way."
