## 1. How a $20M roofing company is actually built

You don't get to $20M with "more AI." You get there by moving four numbers:

**Revenue = (Leads) × (Inspection rate) × (Close rate) × (Avg job value) × (Reps producing) — minus what leaks out of missed calls, slow follow-up, and unramped reps.**

So the whole system attacks exactly those leak points:

| Lever | Who owns it | Effect |
|---|---|---|
| More qualified leads | Demand engine (Google/Meta/storm/SEO) | Top of funnel |
| Never miss a lead | AI Receptionist (Agent 1) + follow-up (Agent 2) | Stop the leak |
| Higher close rate | Trained reps + AI coach (Agent 3) | Mid funnel |
| More reps producing | Hiring funnel + recruiting AI (Agent 4) | Multiply capacity |
| Faster claims/installs | Claims AI (Agent 5) + production | Throughput + cash |

## 2. The model (stated assumptions — change these to your real numbers)

These are transparent industry-grounded estimates for DMV insurance/retail roofing. **Replace with UD's actuals** as we get them; the structure is what matters.

**Assumptions:**
- Average job value (roof, often w/ gutters/siding): **$14,000**
- Current revenue: **~$10M** → ≈ **715 jobs/year** (~60/month)
- Inspection → signed close rate today: **~30%** (untrained/mixed reps, some missed leads)
- Today's productive reps: assume **~8–10**

**Today's implied funnel (annual):**
```
~2,400 booked inspections → 30% close → ~715 jobs × $14K ≈ $10M
```

**To reach $20M we need ≈ 1,430 jobs/year (~715 more).** Three ways to get them, used together:

### Lever A — Stop the leak (fastest, cheapest)
Missed calls + slow follow-up commonly lose **20–30%** of inbound in home services. Capturing even **half** of currently-lost leads with Agent 1 + Agent 2:
- +~360 inspections → +~110 jobs → **+~$1.5M** with essentially zero new ad spend.

### Lever B — Raise close rate (training)
Move close rate from **30% → 40%** via the academy + AI coach, on the same inspection volume (~2,760 after Lever A):
- 2,760 × 40% = ~1,104 jobs vs. 828 → **+~276 jobs → +~$3.9M.**

### Lever C — More leads + more reps (scale)
Demand engine adds inspection volume; hiring adds the reps to work it. To clear the remaining gap to ~1,430 jobs we need inspection volume up to ~**3,600/yr** at 40% close:
- 3,600 × 40% = **1,440 jobs × $14K ≈ $20.2M.**
- Going from ~2,400 → ~3,600 inspections = **+50% qualified lead volume** (Google + Meta + storm + SEO).
- That throughput needs roughly **15–20 productive reps** (vs ~8–10 today) → that's why hiring + 2–4 week ramp is non-negotiable.

### Scenario summary
| Scenario | Leads/yr | Close % | Jobs | Revenue |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Today | ~2,400 | 30% | ~715 | ~$10.0M |
| Year 1 base | ~3,000 | 36% | ~1,080 | **~$15.1M** |
| Year 1–2 target | ~3,600 | 40% | ~1,440 | **~$20.2M** |

**$20M is reachable — but it's a leads + conversion + headcount problem solved together, over 12–24 months, not a switch.**

## 3. The honest constraints (what has to be true)

A real agency tells you the risks, not just the upside. Doubling revenue means:

1. **Production capacity must double too.** Twice the signed jobs needs the crews, materials, and project management to *install* them. If sales outruns production, you get the exact failure mode in UD's one public complaint (slow damage follow-through). **Scale sales and production in lockstep** — model crew capacity alongside the sales funnel.
2. **Cash flow.** Insurance jobs pay in ACV→RCV stages. Faster volume = more working capital tied up. Claims AI (Agent 5) + tight collections matter as much as sales.
3. **Reps are the bottleneck, not leads.** You can't buy your way to $20M with ad spend if reps can't close or you can't ramp them. Hiring + training is the throttle.
4. **Storm dependency.** Storm years vary. The demand engine must also win *non-storm* retail/search demand so revenue isn't purely weather-driven.
5. **License & reputation are the real moat.** Every claim must be legitimate and documented. One regulatory issue or a wave of bad reviews undoes years of growth. The 131+ five-star reputation is an asset to protect, not risk.

> Bottom line: the plan is aggressive but real. The governor is **production capacity + rep ramp**, and we build to respect both.
