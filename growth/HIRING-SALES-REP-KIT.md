# UD Sales Rep Hiring Kit — post this week
**Why this exists:** the Master Growth Plan's math requires 15–20 producing reps to hit $20M. Leads without reps = wasted storms. This kit is copy-paste ready.

## 1. Indeed/ZipRecruiter job post (copy-paste)

**Title:** Storm Restoration Sales Rep — $80K–$160K OTE, W-2 or 1099, Leads Provided (Bethesda/Alexandria)

United Developers is a family-owned, GAF Factory-Certified roofing & storm-restoration company serving MD/VA since 2005 — A+ BBB, 4.9★ across 131+ Google reviews. We're expanding our sales team because our lead engine (storm-verified canvassing zones, inbound web leads, AI receptionist, commercial accounts) produces more appointments than our current team can run.

**What you'll do:** run company-provided inspection appointments + canvass storm-verified neighborhoods (we hand you the NWS-verified map, not a guess) · inspect and photo-document roofs (we train you) · guide homeowners through insurance claims the legal way — we document, the adjuster decides · close with financing options ($0-down, 12–180 months).

**What you get:** commission 8–12% of contract value (avg job $14K → $1,100–$1,700 per roof) · realistic year-1: $80K–$120K; proven closers: $160K+ · 2-week paid training academy (sales playbook, flashcards, field kit, ride-alongs) · leads + tech stack provided (address intel tool, instant quotes, claim packets — you sell, software does the paperwork) · growth path: senior rep → team lead → sales manager as we scale.

**You:** coachable, show up, comfortable knocking doors after storms, clean driving record, smartphone. Roofing/D2D/insurance restoration experience is a plus, not required — our best reps started green. Spanish/Hindi/Russian bilingual a major plus.

Apply with a 1-minute note on why you'll outwork everyone. Interviews this week.

## 2. Screen (15-min phone) — 5 questions, score 1–5 each
1. "Walk me through the hardest thing you've sold or convinced someone of." (story quality)
2. "A homeowner says 'my roof is fine, I'm not filing a claim.' What do you say?" (listens vs. argues)
3. "What does your typical Tuesday look like right now?" (work ethic signal)
4. "Commission-heavy comp — what's your monthly number you must hit and why?" (motivation realism)
5. "Storm hits Saturday night. What do you want from us Sunday morning?" (initiative — best answer: the map and a partner)
≥20/25 → ride-along interview. The ride-along IS the interview: 2 hours knocking with a lead rep; watch effort, not polish.

## 3. Comp plan (put in writing, signed)
- 10% of collected contract value (8% if company-set appointment, 12% if self-generated), paid on collection — never on signing.
- Training period: 2 weeks @ $500/wk draw against commissions.
- Compliance clause (termination offense): paying/waiving deductibles, promising claim outcomes, misrepresenting damage, knocking no-solicit homes/DC without license.
- Review-getting bonus: $25/5-star Google review naming the rep.

## 4. Ramp (weeks 1–2)
Day 1–2: training deck + flashcards (training/) · Day 3–4: ride-alongs · Day 5: roof safety + photo documentation + Command Center training (pipeline, intel, quotes, packets) · Week 2: knocks with partner, runs 2 supervised inspections, first solo close target by day 14.

Solar D2D reps burning out (same skill, better product story) · servers/bartenders/gym trainers (talk + hustle) · the careers page (live) + a GBP post · referral bounty: $500 to any employee whose referral closes 3 roofs.
