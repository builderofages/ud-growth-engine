Non-negotiable red flags (auto-pass)

🚩 Late to the interview · can't explain a time they pushed through rejection · badmouths a past employer/customer · "I just need something for now" · won't do the role-play · no reliable transportation (field roles) · dodgy on background/license questions.

Interview flow (20–25 min)

1
Hook (2m): sell the opportunity — uncapped income, we bring leads, growing company. A-players have options; make them want it.
2
Story (5m): “Tell me about a time you got told no over and over and kept going.” Listen for grit, not excuses.
3
Numbers (3m): “What did you earn last year, and what do you want to earn?” A-players want more and own their results.
4
Role-play (8m) — THE filter: hand them the phone or door script and run a live mock. Setters: cold-call you. Canvassers: knock the “door.” Reps: pitch you the deductible offer. Coachability + composure under pressure shows here.
5
Close (3m): “If we offer you this today, can you start Monday and hit the ground running?” Watch for hesitation/excuses.

Best questions to separate elite from average

QuestionA-player answer sounds like…
“Walk me through your best sales month ever.”Specific numbers, what they did differently, owns it.
“How do you handle a homeowner who slams the door?”Unbothered, next door, has a system — not personal.
“What do you do when you're behind on your number Friday?”Works the weekend, more activity — not “it happens.”
“Teach me something in 60 seconds.”Clear, confident, engaging — that's their pitch ability.
