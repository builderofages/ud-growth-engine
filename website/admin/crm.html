Comp model (storm/insurance resi)

Setters: hourly + per-booked-inspection bonus. Reps: commission on gross profit (typical 8–12% of job, or ~$750–$1,500/closed job) — uncapped. Production manager: salary + completion bonus. Supplement specialist: % of supplement dollars recovered. Keep reps' pipeline visible here so comp is transparent and disputes vanish.

⚡ Speed-to-lead SLA (the #1 conversion lever)

1
<5 minutes: text + call every new lead. Contact rates collapse after 5 min. The CRM flags hot leads on the dashboard.
2
<15 minutes: if no answer, leave a voicemail + send the inspection-booking text. Move to “Contacted.”
3
Day 1–7: 6 touches (call/text/email) before giving up. Most sales happen on touch 3–5.

The 5-step job process

1
Free 24-hr inspection — full photo report, no obligation.
2
Document & meet the adjuster on-site — nothing legitimate missed.
3
Claim approved — file supplements for code upgrades & missed items.
4
Install — most roofs in one day.
5
Pay deductible + ask for the review & referral — same day, every job.

Daily cadence (you + son)

AM
Check new leads, assign reps, fire any review requests from yesterday's completed jobs. Check the storm tracker.
PM
Move pipeline cards, log notes, export a backup CSV. After a storm: run the canvassing walk-list and send reps.
⚖️ Compliance: in MD/VA contractors may not waive/pay/rebate a homeowner's deductible, and may not negotiate the claim itself (that's public adjusting). Document damage, meet the adjuster — never "handle the negotiation."
