The no-show killer: 3-touch confirmation cadence

Confirmed appointments show up. This is the single fix for “my rep drove out and nobody was home.” Run it on EVERY booked inspection.
1
At booking (instant): send the confirmation text the moment the time is set. Get a “yes 👍” reply.
2
Day before (evening): reminder text with the window + a one-tap reschedule option. This catches conflicts before the truck rolls.
3
Morning of (2–3 hrs prior): “on our way” text with the rep’s name. Final lock-in.
📊 Benchmark: a 3-touch confirmation cadence cuts roofing inspection no-shows from ~30–40% down to ~10%. That's the difference between a wasted drive and a sit.
⚖️ Send these as manual / 1:1 texts from a real number (or your CRM/GHL with the lead's consent from the form). Avoid blasting un-consented numbers with an autodialer — TCPA still requires prior express written consent for automated texts.

Inbound / callback phone script (setter)

Open (call within 5 min)“Hi, is this {Name}? This is ___ with United Developers — you just asked about your roof online, I’m calling you right back. Did I catch you okay?”
Qualify“Got it. So I can send the right specialist — is this for storm/hail damage, a leak, or a full replacement? … And roughly when did you first notice it?”
Book (assume the appointment)“Perfect. The inspection is free and takes about 45 minutes — you don’t need to be on the roof, we document everything with photos. I’ve got Thursday at 10 or Friday at 2 — which is easier?”
Lock + confirm“You’re set for {day/time}. I’m texting you a confirmation right now — reply ‘yes’ so I know you got it. We’ll also remind you the day before. Anything I should tell the specialist before they arrive?”

Door-to-door script (canvasser — storm zones)

⚖️ Before knocking: have the county/city solicitor permit, skip any home with a posted “No Soliciting” sign (enforceable in VA), and never disparage the insurer or promise to “waive the deductible.”
Open (non-salesy)“Hey, sorry to bother you — I’m ___ with United Developers, we’re the local roofers working this street after the storm last week. We’re finding hail damage on a lot of these roofs that you can’t see from the ground. Did anyone check yours yet?”
Build (curiosity + proof)“We just got off your neighbor’s roof two doors down — same age roof, real damage. Hail doesn’t skip houses. The check’s free and takes 15 minutes from a ladder — worst case you find out you’re fine.”
Book on the doorstep (the goal)“I can have our specialist out tomorrow morning or afternoon — what’s better for you? … Great, what’s the best cell to text the confirmation to? … And your name spelled correctly?”
If insurance objection“Totally — and to be clear, we don’t handle your claim or your money. We document the damage and meet your adjuster so nothing legit gets missed. Most folks just pay their deductible. The inspection itself costs you nothing.”

Legal guardrails — MD · VA · DC · PA (June 2026)

Real rules so you scale without complaints/fines. Not legal advice — confirm specifics with counsel, but this keeps reps on the right side.
TopicRule / what to do
Door-to-door (MD)Maryland Door-to-Door Sales Act: homeowner has the right to cancel until midnight of the 5th business day (7th if elderly). Put the cancellation notice in every contract. Local solicitor permits often required (county/city).
Door-to-door (VA)Posted “No Soliciting” signs are legally enforcing — ignoring one is a Class 1 misdemeanor (VA Code §18.2-119). Skip those homes. Counties (Fairfax, etc.) require a peddler/solicitor license — get it before knocking.
Calling / texting (TCPA)The FCC one-to-one consent rule was vacated Feb 2025 — your own lead-form consent (with clear disclosure) is valid. BUT automated/autodialed calls & texts still need prior express written consent. Manual dials to numbers not on the DNC list are the safe path for setters. Scrub against the National DNC Registry for cold outbound.
Insurance / deductibleIn MD & VA contractors may not pay, waive, or rebate a homeowner's deductible, and may not negotiate the claim (that's unlicensed public adjusting). Document damage, meet the adjuster — never "handle the claim/negotiation." Keep this line in sales training.
D.C. service⚠️ The site advertises D.C. service but lists MD/VA/PA licenses only. D.C. requires its own DLCP contractor license. Either get it or scope marketing to MD/VA. (Flagged in the audit.)
Contracts & AOBUse a clear written contract; avoid aggressive Assignment-of-Benefits clauses that regulators scrutinize. Itemize scope; no "contingency on insurance approval" language that crosses into claims handling.
