How much does a roof replacement cost in Montgomery County, MD?

Updated June 2026 · United Developers · 6-minute read · Bethesda · Rockville · Silver Spring · Gaithersburg · Potomac · Germantown

The short answer for 2026: most full asphalt-shingle roof replacements in Montgomery County land between $4.00 and $8.50 per square foot installed — roughly $10,000 to $25,000 for a typical single-family home. That's a wide range, and where your house falls inside it comes down to a handful of measurable factors, not negotiation skill. Here's the honest breakdown, plus the one scenario where the number on your check shrinks to just your insurance deductible.

2026 cost ranges at a glance

What actually drives the price

  • Roof size and pitch. Pricing is per square foot of roof surface, not floor area. Steep or cut-up roofs (hips, valleys, dormers — common in Potomac and Bethesda) need more material, more labor, and more safety setup.
  • Material. Architectural shingles (like GAF Timberline HDZ) are the county standard at roughly $4.00–$8.50/sq ft installed. Standing-seam metal runs two to three times that but lasts 40–70 years.
  • Tear-off and decking. Removing one old layer is standard; if the plywood underneath has rot or the home has two existing layers, expect line-item additions discovered at tear-off.
  • Permits and HOA review. Montgomery County permit fees are among the higher in the region (often $300+), and many communities in Germantown, Rockville and North Potomac require HOA color/material approval before work starts.
  • Ventilation and code items. Older Silver Spring and Wheaton homes often need ridge-vent or intake upgrades to meet current code and keep shingle warranties valid.

Rule of thumb: take your home's footprint, add roughly 10–30% for pitch and overhangs, and multiply by $4.00–$8.50. A 2,400 sq ft roof at $6/sq ft ≈ $14,400. For an exact number in 60 seconds, use our instant satellite roof quote.

The scenario where you pay only your deductible

Montgomery County sits squarely in the DMV's hail and wind corridor. If a covered storm damaged your roof, your homeowners policy — not your savings — typically pays for the replacement, and your out-of-pocket cost is usually just your deductible. The catch is documentation: insurers cover sudden storm damage, not age or wear, so the damage has to be photographed and tied to a specific storm date.

Ways to keep the cost down (without cutting corners)

  • Check the storm path first. An approved claim turns a $15,000 project into a deductible-sized expense.
  • Replace in shoulder season. Spring and fall book up fastest in the county; late fall and winter scheduling can be more flexible.
  • Don't overlay. A second shingle layer over the old one looks cheaper today but voids most manufacturer warranties, hides deck rot, and costs more at the next replacement.
  • Use financing instead of downgrading materials. $0-down plans with 12–180 month terms keep a quality architectural roof affordable as a monthly payment — see the financing calculator.
  • Verify the license. Anyone quoting suspiciously low should still hold a Maryland MHIC license and local references — our contractor checklist covers the red flags.

How Montgomery County compares

Per-square-foot pricing is similar across the DMV, but Montgomery County totals trend higher than the regional average simply because homes are larger and roof designs more complex — a Bethesda colonial with dormers carries more roof area than a comparable rambler in Prince George's County. Frequently asked questions

How much does a roof replacement cost in Montgomery County?

Roughly $4.00–$8.50 per square foot installed — about $10,000–$25,000 for a typical single-family home. Larger or more complex roofs in Bethesda and Potomac can exceed that. Final pricing follows a free on-site inspection.

Why is Montgomery County pricier than other parts of Maryland?

Larger homes, more complex roof designs, higher county permit fees (often $300+), and HOA design requirements in many neighborhoods all push totals toward the upper half of Maryland's range.

Will insurance pay for my roof?

If a covered storm (hail or wind) damaged it, yes — typically you pay only your deductible. Age and wear aren't covered, so prompt photo documentation tied to a storm date is what makes a claim work.

How long does the job take?

Most full residential replacements are completed in one day, weather permitting. County permitting usually adds a few days to two weeks before the install date.
